Presence is Power
Client
Apara / Implus
Role
Brand Strategy · Copywriting
Deliverables
Brand Architecture · Manifesto · Core Values · Brand Line
Problem
Implus was looking to expand into the yoga market by launching a new brand, Apara. Our task was to conceptualize a brand from the ground-up to present to the C-suite.
Insight
We saw a gap in the yoga market between the ultra-skinny, aesthetic-driven brands like Lululemon and Alo and the more mom-coded brands like Athlete and Prana. We wanted to target athletic women on a mission to move, and only use real women in our advertising. We didn't want to be aspirational, we wanted to be allies.
Solution
With extensive empathy and perception mapping, we created a complete brand book, manifesto, and core values. We then partnered with a creative agency to build a video teaser with the manifesto read out loud. Unfortunately, the sudden rise of COVID-19 prevented the brand's existence.

SKLZ Product Copy
Three creative territories for product positioning
Client
SKLZ / Implus
Role
Copy Strategy · Creative Direction
Deliverables
Three Creative Territories · Tonal Direction · Sample Copy
Problem
SKLZ needed to update its product copy but hadn't aligned internally on what its brand voice should sound like. Without a clear tonal direction, any copy refresh risked feeling inconsistent, or defaulting to the same generic sports-brand language the category over utilized.
Insight
The target consumers were parents purchasing products for their kids, so we needed to appeal to both demographics by testing copy that directly addressed both audiences. We knew that parents cared about ease and kids cared about their sport, so we tried to include both in the copy.
Solution
Different tones speak to fundamentally different athletes. A feature-driven, adjective-heavy approach appeals to the gear-focused buyer, while a verb-driven, benefit-focused voice motivates the results-oriented athlete. Giving SKLZ a side-by-side comparison made the choice concrete rather than abstract.

Trilogy Sanctuary
We Are a Way of Life
Client
Trilogy Sanctuary, San Diego
Role
Brand Strategy · Copywriting
Deliverables
Brand Book · Manifesto · Core Values · Voice & Tone · Strategic Deliverables
Problem
Trilogy Sanctuary, San Diego's highest-grossing wellness studio, had grown rapidly but lacked a formal brand foundation or studio standards. With multiple teachers, expanding offerings, and a passionate but undefined community, the studio needed a brand book and clear studio guidelines that could align everyone on what Trilogy truly stood for.
Insight
Trilogy's power wasn't in its classes, it was in the feeling it created. Students didn't just come to practice yoga, they came to transform. We needed to allow teachers to retain their unique offerings and creativity while clearly defining what to expect from class to better aide first-time clients.
Solution
The brand was positioned at the intersection of spiritual depth and creative embodiment: aspirational, meaningful, and unlike anything else in the San Diego market. We built the complete brand book from scratch: brand manifesto, foundation statement, core values, personality, identity, and strategic positioning. We also created a series of strategic deliverables that aligned the new brand vision with specific objectives to improve the yoga program.

Live Free. Stay Wild.
Client
Rick Huth / ruk-media
Role
Copywriting · Brand Strategy · SEO
Deliverables
Brand Audit · Tagline · Mission Statement · Website Copy · SEO Strategy
Problem
Rick Huth, an outdoor adventure videographer and photographer based in Southern California, had a working website but no cohesive brand voice while pitching his work to clients.
Insight
Rick doesn't just shoot content, he goes on the adventure with you. His value isn't technical skill alone, but that he's the kind of shooter who'll hike into the backcountry, ride the mountain, and find the shot in real time. The brand needed to speak to people who refuse to be captured any other way.
Solution
Conducted a full brand audit covering voice, positioning, naming, tagline, SEO keywords, and core values. Rewrote the mission statement, developed three tagline directions, and copyedited all service and package descriptions to give Rick a sharper brand presence. All work was built for his target audience of outdoor brands, social media creators, and adventure athletes.
